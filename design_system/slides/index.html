§ 01 · why we care

Three things to notice.

  • Wide networks fit the training set easily — but in a very specific order.
  • That order is set by the NTK eigenvalue decay.
  • This is testable. We test it. It holds.
a.c 03 / 07
§ 02 · the bound

Loss decay, by eigenmode.

For mode k with NTK eigenvalue λk, the residual at step t decays as:

$$ r_k(t) \;\le\; r_k(0) \cdot \exp\!\bigl(- \eta \lambda_k t\bigr). $$

Modes with large λk — the low-frequency ones — collapse first. Modes with small λk stay around. This is the whole spectral-bias story, in one line.

a.c 04 / 07
§ 03 · before / after

Prior bound vs. ours.

prior work
O(λₖ · t)

Loose for large k. Predicts decay everywhere but doesn't capture the log-k correction.

ours
O(λₖ · t / log k)

Tight under standard regularity. Matches the empirical curves across four decades.
