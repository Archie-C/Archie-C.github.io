You were right — the old purple was fighting the cream. The cream is warm (yellow-leaning, around 75° hue); the old #5B2A86 was cool (blue-violet, 305°). On a warm ground that produced the classic simultaneous-contrast vibration — purple looked slightly cold and synthetic, cream looked slightly muddy. I've rotated the accent ~25° warmer into aubergine (#6B2C5C, hue ~335°) — still unmistakably purple, but with enough red in it to sit comfortably on cream the way ink sits on antique paper.
