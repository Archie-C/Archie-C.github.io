On the spectral bias of wide neural networks

Archie Carpenter1, Jihye Park2, Ren Saito1

1Department of Applied Mathematics  ·  2Institute for Statistical Learning

Read the paper ↗ arXiv ↗ code ↗ BibTeX slides
tl;dr Wide networks learn low-frequency components of the target function before high-frequency ones — and the rate is set by the eigenvalue decay of the NTK. We give a closed-form characterisation, a tighter bound than prior work, and a clean experimental confirmation on five standard benchmarks.

Abstract

In the infinite-width limit, training a neural network with gradient descent is equivalent to kernel regression with the neural tangent kernel (NTK). A consequence — observed empirically by many but, to our knowledge, never tightly bounded — is that the low-frequency components of the target function are learned first. We make this statement precise: we give a closed-form expression for the rate at which each eigenmode of the NTK is fit, and we show that the prior bound of O(λ_k t) can be tightened to O(λ_k t / log k) under standard regularity assumptions. We verify the prediction on five benchmark tasks across image and audio domains.
